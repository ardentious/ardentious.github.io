Sensor Showdown: The Heart of the Ride

The sensor dictates how the motor responds to you. For precise trail control and photography, your choice matters.

Torque Sensor

💪

Measures your effort. The harder you pedal, the more power you get. It's an intuitive, seamless amplification of your own strength.

Cadence Sensor

🔄

Detects if pedals are moving, then gives a set amount of power. Can feel like an on/off switch.

A Torque Sensor is highly recommended for its precise control—perfect for navigating trails and setting up photos.

Class Comparison

Classes define speed limits and throttle rules. Click each class to see the details.

Class 1: Pedal-Assist

Motor helps only when you pedal. Max speed: 20 mph. No throttle.

Class 2: Throttle on Demand

Has a throttle, and helps when you pedal. Max speed: 20 mph.

Class 3: Speed

Motor helps when you pedal. Max speed: 28 mph. Throttle (if present) usually capped at 20 mph.

Your ideal bike is a configurable Class 2/3 model, giving you 28 mph pedal-assist for speed and a 20 mph throttle for convenience.

Rules of the Road & Trail

Responsible riding means knowing the law. Your e-bike is subject to different rules on state roads versus federal forest land.

  • ✔️No License Required: You do not need a driver's license, registration, or insurance for your e-bike in Montana.
  • ✔️General Access: E-bikes are typically allowed on roads and paths where traditional bikes go.
  • ⚠️Evolving Laws: Montana is considering formally adopting the 3-class system. A configurable bike ensures you can adapt to any new regulations.

Critical Distinction: E-bikes are considered MOTORIZED vehicles on National Forest land.

  • 🗺️MVUM is Law: You can ONLY ride on roads and trails designated open to motor vehicles on the official Motor Vehicle Use Map (MVUM).
  • ❌"Casual Trails" Are Likely Off-Limits: If a trail is not on the MVUM as motorized, it is prohibited. This includes hiking trails, game trails, and Wilderness areas.
  • 🐻Be Prepared: Always download MVUMs for offline use (Avenza Maps app), and be Bear Aware.
